ABN vs TFN — understand the difference

TFN (Employee)

Tax File Number

  • You are an employee
  • Employer deducts tax (PAYG) at source
  • No GST to worry about
  • Right to holidays, sick leave, super
  • Stable, less freedom
ABN (Sole Trader)

Australian Business Number

  • You are your own boss
  • YOU set aside tax (~25-32% gross)
  • GST mandatory if billing > $75k/year
  • Can deduct business expenses
  • Freedom, more responsibility

Most migrants need both: TFN for casual work (e.g. hospitality), ABN for side gigs (e.g. cleaning, rideshare, freelance).

Tax #1 — GST (Goods & Services Tax)

15% that is NOT yours

If you bill as ABN more than $75,000 per year, you must register for GST.

What this means in practice

  • You charge +10% on each invoice
  • That 10% is NOT yours — it belongs to the government
  • You report and pay it via BAS (Business Activity Statement) quarterly
  • You can claim GST credits on business purchases (input tax credits)
Common mistake: migrant receives $75k of gross invoices and spends EVERYTHING. When BAS arrives, discovers $5-7k of GST owed and can't pay. SET ASIDE 10% from each payment from day 1.
07 / 18
Tax #2 — PAYG (Pay As You Go)

Income tax no one deducts for you

Unlike TFN where the employer deducts tax from the payslip, with ABN YOU are responsible for setting aside income tax.

Brackets for 2025-26 (Australian residents)

  • Up to $18,200/year: 0% (tax-free threshold)
  • $18,201 to $45,000: 16%
  • $45,001 to $135,000: 30%
  • $135,001 to $190,000: 37%
  • Above $190,000: 45%

Practical rule: set aside 25-32% of each payment in a separate account. End of year, pay ATO with that money.

Part 3 — For student visa holders

The 48-hour rule

48h

Student visa holders can only work up to 48 hours every 14 days during study periods.

Important: exceeded = visa cancellation. Department of Home Affairs cross-references data with ATO via TFN/ABN. They KNOW how much you worked.

How to track

  • Count HOURS, not days — 14-day rolling window (not calendar)
  • Each casual gig, rideshare, cleaning counts
  • Volunteer work does NOT count (but must be proven)
  • University on break: unlimited hours allowed

Apps like Ozly have Visa Shield — automatic alert when you approach the limit.

10 deductions ABN sole traders forget

  1. Fuel for vehicle used for work
  2. Mileage in personal vehicle (cents/km)
  3. Phone bill (% of professional use)
  4. Home internet (% of professional use)
  5. Equipment bought for work (up to $1,000 immediate)
  6. Software / apps (yes, this includes Ozly)
  7. Training and courses related to profession
  8. Portion of rent if working from home office
  9. Accountant / tax agent (fee is deductible)
  10. Professional insurance (Public Liability, etc)
Golden rule: no receipt = no deduction. Photo of receipt on day of purchase, categorised. ATO can audit up to 5 years later.
